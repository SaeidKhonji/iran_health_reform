# Iran Health Reform

A collaborative knowledge repository for analyzing and improving Iran's healthcare system through systems thinking.

## Purpose

This repository aims to turn scattered experiences, problems, and ideas into a shared, reviewable, and evolving body of knowledge.

## Core idea

Healthcare problems should not be reduced to conflict between patient and doctor, or to isolated issues like tariffs, insurance, or income.

They should be studied as parts of a complex system involving:

- patients
- physicians
- nurses
- insurance organizations
- government
- medical education
- professional ethics
- data and digital infrastructure
- public trust

## Current focus

The first focus areas are:

1. Patient–doctor trust
2. Insurance and payment reform
3. Electronic health records
4. Medical AI as decision support
5. Quotas and educational justice
6. Physician burnout and mental health
7. Transparency and quality evaluation

## How to contribute

You can contribute by:

- recording lived experiences
- opening issues
- joining discussions
- suggesting edits
- adding evidence, data, and references
- proposing practical reforms

## Principle

The goal is not blame, anger, or revenge.

The goal is to transform individual pain into collective understanding, and collective understanding into institutional reform.
